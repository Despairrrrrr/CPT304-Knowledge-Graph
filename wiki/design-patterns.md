# Design Patterns Overview

## Overview

Design patterns are typical solutions to commonly occurring problems in software design. They are like pre-made blueprints that can be customized to solve recurring design problems — not specific code to copy-paste, but general concepts for solving particular problems.

---

## Classification

All patterns can be categorized by their intent or purpose:

| Category | Focus | Examples |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **Creational** | Object creation mechanisms that increase flexibility and reuse | Factory, Builder, Singleton |
| **Structural** | Assembling objects and classes into larger, flexible structures | Decorator, Adapter, Proxy, Composite |
| **Behavioral** | Effective communication and assignment of responsibilities between objects | Chain of Responsibility, Strategy, State, Observer |

---

## Benefits

- **Reusability**: Solutions reusable across multiple projects
- **Best Practices**: Reflects experience and insights of industry professionals
- **Understandability & Maintainability**: Consistent, well-documented approach
- **Communication**: Provides a common vocabulary for team discussions

## Drawbacks

- **Overuse**: Using patterns unnecessarily adds complexity
- **Initial Overhead**: Learning curve slows initial development
- **Dependence**: Over-reliance can limit innovative thinking
